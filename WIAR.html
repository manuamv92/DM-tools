What is a tabletop role-playing game (RPG)?

Tabletop RPGs are interactive story-telling games played around a tabletop. Players assume the roles of characters and act out their actions within a fictional setting, using dice to determine the outcome of certain actions. The game is guided by a Game Master (DM), who creates the setting, story, and arbitrates the rules.

How do you play a tabletop RPG?

To play a tabletop RPG, you will need a group of friends and a set of dice. One player, the DM, creates a setting and story, and guides the other players through the game. The other players create their own characters and take turns making decisions and acting out their character's actions. The DM arbitrates the rules and resolves conflicts within the game world.

What do you need to play a tabletop RPG?

In addition to a group of friends and dice, you will need a rulebook or guidelines for the specific game you are playing like SRD5. Some games also require character sheets to keep track of your character's abilities and stats. Pen and paper, or a computer, can be helpful for keeping track of game progress and notes. In some games you may also want to have miniature figures or other visual aids to help you visualize the game world.

What are some popular tabletop RPGs?

Some popular tabletop RPGs include Dungeons and Dragons, Pathfinder, and Shadowrun. There are also many other types of RPGs, such as live action role-playing games (LARPs) and video game RPGs. Some popular video game RPGs include The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, Mass Effect, and Fallout 4.
